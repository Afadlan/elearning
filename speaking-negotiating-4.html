🎤 Speaking Task 7 - Read Aloud

Instructions: Read the following international business negotiation. Show cultural sensitivity and diplomatic tone.

Cross-Cultural Business Partnership

"Good afternoon, Mr. Tanaka. It's an honor to meet with you and discuss this potential partnership between our companies. I understand that in Japanese business culture, building trust and long-term relationships is paramount, and I want to assure you that we share these values. Regarding the proposed joint venture, I recognize that our initial timeline of six months may seem rushed from your perspective. In Western business, we often focus on quick results, but I appreciate that your company values thorough planning and consensus-building. Would it be acceptable if we extended the planning phase to nine months? This would allow both teams to develop a more comprehensive strategy and ensure that all stakeholders are comfortable with the arrangement. We believe that taking the time to do this properly will lead to a more successful and sustainable partnership."

Show respect for cultural differences. Use diplomatic language and demonstrate cultural awareness.

🎤 Speaking Task 8 - Read Aloud

Instructions: Read the following conflict resolution dialogue. Show empathy and problem-solving skills.

Team Conflict Resolution

Manager: "Thank you both for coming. I know there have been some tensions between your departments lately, and I'd like us to work together to find a solution."

Department A: "The issue is that Department B keeps changing requirements at the last minute, which makes it impossible for us to meet our deadlines."

Department B: "We understand your frustration, but we're responding to client feedback. We need to be flexible to meet customer needs."

Manager: "I can see both perspectives here. What if we established a change request process with a forty-eight-hour notice period? This would give Department A time to adjust while still allowing Department B to respond to client needs. We could also schedule weekly coordination meetings to anticipate potential changes. Would this approach work for both teams?"

Show each person's perspective clearly. Demonstrate the manager's neutral, solution-focused approach.
