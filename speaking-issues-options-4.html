🎤 Speaking Task 7 - Read Aloud

Instructions: Read the following crisis management scenario. Show urgency and systematic crisis response.

Supply Chain Crisis Response

"We're facing a critical supply chain disruption that threatens our entire production schedule. Our main supplier in Southeast Asia has been affected by severe flooding, and they won't be able to deliver essential components for at least six weeks. This crisis impacts seventy percent of our product lines and could result in significant revenue losses if we don't act immediately. We need to implement emergency measures while developing both short-term and long-term solutions. First, let's assess our current inventory levels and identify which products we can continue manufacturing with existing stock. Second, we must immediately contact alternative suppliers, even if their prices are higher than our usual costs. Third, we should consider temporarily modifying our product specifications to use available components from different suppliers. Fourth, we need to communicate transparently with our customers about potential delays and offer alternative solutions or compensation. Additionally, we should explore the possibility of sourcing components from local suppliers to reduce future dependency on international supply chains. We must also evaluate our insurance coverage for supply chain disruptions and consider implementing a more diversified supplier network to prevent similar crises in the future. Time is critical, so we need to make decisions quickly while ensuring we maintain product quality and customer satisfaction throughout this challenging period."

Read with urgency and authority. Show systematic crisis management approach and decisive leadership.

🎤 Speaking Task 8 - Read Aloud

Instructions: Read the following innovation dialogue. Show creative thinking and collaborative problem-solving.

Innovation Workshop - Creative Solutions

Innovation Director: "Today's challenge is to find creative solutions for reducing our company's carbon footprint while maintaining profitability. Let's think outside the box and explore unconventional approaches."

Engineer: "What if we redesigned our packaging using biodegradable materials made from agricultural waste? This could reduce costs and environmental impact simultaneously."

Marketing Specialist: "That's interesting! We could also create a customer loyalty program where people earn points for returning used packaging for recycling. This would engage customers in our sustainability efforts."

Innovation Director: "Excellent ideas! Let's also consider implementing a four-day work week to reduce energy consumption in our offices. We could use the fifth day for remote work or professional development. Additionally, we might partner with local renewable energy companies to power our facilities with solar or wind energy. Another innovative approach could be developing a product-as-a-service model, where customers lease our products instead of buying them, allowing us to maintain and upgrade items rather than producing new ones constantly. These solutions require initial investment but could generate long-term savings and competitive advantages while significantly reducing our environmental impact."

Show creative enthusiasm and collaborative innovation. Use inspiring and forward-thinking tone.
