Reading Comprehension - Answering the Phone (B2)

Read the phone conversation below and answer questions 1-5.

Phone Conversation at Global Tech Solutions

Receptionist: Good morning, Global Tech Solutions. This is Sarah speaking. How may I help you today?

Caller: Hello, I'm calling to inquire about your software development services. My name is Michael Johnson from Innovate Corp.

Receptionist: Good morning, Mr. Johnson. I'd be happy to help you with that. Are you looking for information about a specific type of software development, or would you like a general overview of our services?

Caller: We're particularly interested in mobile app development for both iOS and Android platforms. We need an app for our customer service department.

Receptionist: That sounds like something our mobile development team would be perfect for. Let me connect you with our project manager, David Chen. He specializes in customer service applications. Would you prefer to speak with him now, or would you like to schedule a call for later?

Caller: If he's available now, that would be great. Otherwise, I'm free tomorrow afternoon after 2 PM.

Receptionist: Let me check his availability. Please hold for just a moment... I'm sorry, Mr. Johnson, but David is currently in a client meeting. However, I can schedule you for a 30-minute consultation tomorrow at 2:30 PM. Would that work for you?

Caller: Perfect! That works well with my schedule. Should I call this number again, or will he call me?

Receptionist: David will call you directly at this number. May I confirm your contact information? I have Michael Johnson from Innovate Corp.

Caller: Yes, that's correct. My direct line is 555-0123, extension 45.

Receptionist: Excellent. I've scheduled your consultation for tomorrow, Tuesday, at 2:30 PM. David will call you at 555-0123, extension 45. Is there anything else I can help you with today?

Caller: No, that covers everything. Thank you so much for your help, Sarah.

Receptionist: You're very welcome, Mr. Johnson. Have a wonderful day, and we look forward to speaking with you tomorrow.

1. What is the main purpose of Michael Johnson's phone call?

2. What specific type of software development is Michael interested in?

3. Why couldn't Michael speak with David Chen immediately?

4. When is the consultation scheduled?

5. What is Michael's direct phone number?
